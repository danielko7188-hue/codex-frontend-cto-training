# Design Scorecard

Use this after audits, redesigns, or comparisons. Score each category from 1 to 5.

## Scoring

- 1: weak
- 2: below standard
- 3: acceptable
- 4: strong
- 5: excellent

## Categories

### 1. Visual hierarchy

- Is the main message obvious immediately?
- Is there one dominant idea per section?
- Does the eye know where to go next?

### 2. Typography

- Does the type feel intentional and premium?
- Are sizes, weights, and line lengths disciplined?
- Does the typography create authority?

### 3. Spacing and rhythm

- Does the page breathe without wasting space?
- Are gaps and section transitions consistent?
- Does the spacing feel deliberate rather than accidental?

### 4. Surface and layout quality

- Does the page rely on layout instead of unnecessary cards?
- Do panels and borders help hierarchy?
- Does the structure feel custom instead of template-derived?

### 5. Trust and credibility

- Would a serious buyer trust this interface?
- Are proof and reassurance visible early enough?
- Does the experience feel safe and competent?

### 6. CTA clarity

- Is the primary next action unmistakable?
- Are there too many competing actions?
- Does each section support conversion or task completion?

### 7. Product fit

- Does the visual language match the business model?
- Does a local-service site feel trustworthy?
- Does a dashboard feel operational instead of marketing-heavy?

### 8. Mobile quality

- Does the hierarchy survive on mobile?
- Are tap targets obvious and comfortable?
- Does the layout remain clean rather than collapsing awkwardly?

## Final readout format

When reporting results, summarize:

- total impression in one sentence
- top 2 strengths
- top 2 weaknesses
- one concrete next improvement
