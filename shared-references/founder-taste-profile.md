# Founder Taste Profile

Last updated: 2026-04-06

Use this file as the current taste source of truth for frontend and UX/UI decisions across projects.

## Core taste

- minimalist
- premium
- professional
- calm instead of loud
- modern without looking trendy for trend's sake
- strong hierarchy
- clear trust signals
- polished but not flashy

## Strong yes

- crisp typography with authority
- restrained color systems
- one strong accent rather than many
- generous but disciplined spacing
- interfaces that feel custom, not template-derived
- layouts with one dominant idea per section
- product surfaces that feel serious and operational
- marketing pages that convert without shouting
- trust-building structure for service businesses
- real proof, real screenshots, real imagery

## Strong no

- generic startup-template look
- purple-on-white AI aesthetic
- card soup
- cluttered dashboards
- oversized empty sections that say little
- shiny/glossy effects with no business purpose
- decorative motion for its own sake
- trend-chasing design that weakens clarity
- landing-page styling pasted onto internal product UI

## Specific founder preference signals already observed

- The founder explicitly said they like minimalist premium professional designs.
- The founder explicitly said they dislike the current frontend design in the remodeling app.
- The founder wants Codex to become a stronger frontend UX/UI expert across many project folders, not just solve one page at a time.

## Translation rules

When in doubt:

- choose restraint over novelty
- choose better typography over more decoration
- choose clearer hierarchy over more features on screen
- choose trust and conversion over aesthetic cleverness
- choose fewer stronger sections over many average sections

## Product-type interpretation

### Marketing sites

- premium means clean, credible, and conversion-aware
- the hero should be strong but not fluffy
- proof should appear early

### Internal SaaS and dashboards

- premium means calm, dense enough, and confident
- rows, lists, and work surfaces beat decorative cards
- the main workspace must dominate the chrome

### Local service businesses

- premium means trustworthy, polished, and easy to contact
- avoid cliche contractor visuals without becoming cold or abstract
- use service clarity, process, reviews, and project evidence to build confidence
