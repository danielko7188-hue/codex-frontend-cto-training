# AI Product UX Scorecard

Score each category from 0 to 10.

## 1. Job Fit

- Is the AI solving a real user problem?
- Is the chosen interaction model right for the job?

## 2. Clarity

- Can a new user tell what the AI does and what to do next?
- Are the main surfaces easy to parse quickly?

## 3. Trust

- Can the user see what the AI used, did, and changed?
- Are risky actions clearly separated from drafts?

## 4. Control

- Can the user interrupt, edit, approve, reject, retry, or recover?
- Are there clear human checkpoints where needed?

## 5. Output Usefulness

- Are outputs rendered in a form that helps the user act?
- Are long responses turned into structured artifacts when appropriate?

## 6. Workflow Integration

- Does the AI fit the real workflow, or does it force everything through chat?
- Is the surrounding product stronger because of the AI?

## 7. State Visibility

- Is it obvious whether the AI is idle, running, waiting, blocked, or done?
- Are background jobs or tool calls understandable?

## 8. Mobile and Responsiveness

- Does the core AI workflow still make sense on smaller screens?
- Are tool results, drafts, and approvals still usable on mobile?

## 9. Visual Restraint

- Does the interface feel premium and serious instead of gimmicky?
- Is the AI UI supporting the workflow rather than showing off?

## 10. Error Recovery

- Are failures understandable?
- Can the user recover without starting over?

## Interpretation

- 9 to 10: excellent
- 7 to 8: strong but still polishable
- 5 to 6: workable but risky
- below 5: redesign the flow, not just the styling
