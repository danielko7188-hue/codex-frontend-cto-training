# Copy And Messaging Pack 2026-04-07

This file maps high-value UX/UI copy jobs to stronger real-world references and practical writing rules.

Its purpose is credibility.

Strong visual design still fails when the words are vague, overhyped, or low-trust.

Use this pack when the layout is fine but the page still feels generic, sales-y, confusing, or AI-written.

## Hero Headline

- Primary anchors: [Vercel](../reference-bank/love/vercel.md), [Linear](../reference-bank/love/linear.md), [Notion Sites](../reference-bank/love/notion-sites.md)
- Best for: homepages, launches, product intros, premium marketing pages
- Borrow: short promise, sharp outcome, category clarity
- Write like:
  - what it is
  - who it is for
  - what better outcome it creates
- Watch out for: abstract slogans that could fit any startup

## Hero Subhead

- Primary anchors: [Mercury](../reference-bank/love/mercury.md), [One Medical](../reference-bank/love/one-medical-home.md), [ServiceTitan](../reference-bank/love/servicetitan-home.md)
- Best for: supporting the hero with practical clarity and trust
- Borrow: plain-English explanation, low hype, real-world context
- Write like:
  - how it works
  - what friction it removes
  - why the promise is believable
- Watch out for: repeating the headline with fluffier words

## Proof Copy

- Primary anchors: [Cloudflare](../reference-bank/love/cloudflare-home.md), [Ramp](../reference-bank/love/ramp-home.md), [ServiceTitan](../reference-bank/love/servicetitan-home.md)
- Best for: stats, case study intros, result callouts, trust bands
- Borrow: named outcomes, meaningful numbers, concrete operational language
- Write like:
  - result + who achieved it + why it matters
  - proof that lowers a real objection
- Watch out for: vanity numbers with no context

## CTA Language

- Primary anchors: [ServiceTitan](../reference-bank/love/servicetitan-home.md), [Airbnb Host Homes](../reference-bank/love/airbnb-host-homes.md), [Vercel](../reference-bank/love/vercel.md)
- Best for: buttons, hero actions, demo asks, lead forms, onboarding starts
- Borrow: clear next step, low ambiguity, low-pressure confidence
- Write like:
  - what the user will actually do next
  - what they get after clicking
- Watch out for: generic verbs like "Learn more" or "Get started" with no context

## Pricing Labels

- Primary anchors: [Notion](../reference-bank/love/notion-home.md), [Stripe](../reference-bank/love/stripe-home.md), [Mercury](../reference-bank/love/mercury-home.md)
- Best for: plan names, billing notes, feature grouping, pricing FAQs
- Borrow: simple naming, fair comparison language, honest scope boundaries
- Write like:
  - who each plan is for
  - what changes when a user upgrades
- Watch out for: clever plan names that hide the difference between tiers

## Form Microcopy

- Primary anchors: [One Medical](../reference-bank/love/one-medical-home.md), [Airbnb Host Homes](../reference-bank/love/airbnb-host-homes.md), [ServiceTitan](../reference-bank/love/servicetitan-home.md)
- Best for: labels, helper text, placeholders, privacy reassurance, multi-step forms
- Borrow: short labels, reassurance near sensitive inputs, expectation-setting
- Write like:
  - why this field is needed
  - what happens after submit
  - how long it will take
- Watch out for: placeholders pretending to be labels, or helper text that says nothing useful

## Empty-State Copy

- Primary anchors: [Notion](../reference-bank/love/notion-home.md), [Cal Teams](../reference-bank/love/cal-teams.md), [Intercom Inbox](../reference-bank/love/intercom-inbox.md)
- Best for: no-data screens, first-run dashboards, empty lists, zero-results states
- Borrow: one next step, one reason to care, one reassuring explanation
- Write like:
  - what is missing
  - why that is okay
  - what to do now
- Watch out for: cute copy that does not help the user move forward

## Settings And Helper Copy

- Primary anchors: [Shopify Polaris](../reference-bank/love/shopify-polaris.md), [Cloudflare](../reference-bank/love/cloudflare-home.md), [Mercury](../reference-bank/love/mercury.md)
- Best for: settings descriptions, permissions, billing details, security explanations
- Borrow: plain labels, calm precision, consequence-aware language
- Write like:
  - what this setting controls
  - what changes when it is enabled
  - what risk or limitation matters
- Watch out for: technical jargon with no user consequence explained

## AI Trust Language

- Primary anchors: [Claude Code](../reference-bank/love/claude-code.md), [ChatGPT Overview](../reference-bank/love/chatgpt-overview.md), [OpenAI Codex](../reference-bank/love/openai-codex.md)
- Best for: AI output framing, tool-call messages, state explanations, review steps
- Borrow: useful confidence, visible limits, explicit user control
- Write like:
  - what the AI is doing
  - what it used
  - what the user can review, edit, retry, or approve
- Watch out for: magical or absolute language that overstates certainty

## Approval And Risk Copy

- Primary anchors: [Claude Code](../reference-bank/love/claude-code.md), [Cloudflare](../reference-bank/love/cloudflare-home.md), [Retool](../reference-bank/love/retool-home.md)
- Best for: confirmations, approvals, destructive actions, compliance review
- Borrow: consequence clarity, calm seriousness, explicit decision framing
- Write like:
  - what will happen
  - what cannot be undone
  - what should be checked first
- Watch out for: vague "Are you sure?" confirmations with no specifics

## Messaging Rules

1. Name the product category early.
2. Trade abstraction for clarity.
3. Make proof do real work, not decorative work.
4. Explain the next step near the action.
5. Use calm confidence instead of hype.
6. For AI products, always explain control and review.
