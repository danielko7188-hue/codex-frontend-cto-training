# Minimalist Premium Founder Pack

Last updated: 2026-04-07

This is the handpicked subset of `awesome-design-md` that best matches the founder taste profile:

- minimalist
- premium
- professional
- calm instead of loud
- polished but not flashy

Use this pack first before browsing the full `awesome-design-md` library.

## Tier 1: Default First Picks

These are the strongest default choices for the founder's taste.

### 1. Vercel

- Local file: [vercel/DESIGN.md](./examples/vercel/DESIGN.md)
- Why it fits: The cleanest translation of premium restraint, disciplined spacing, sharp typography, and serious product polish.
- Best for: premium SaaS marketing pages, developer tools, AI product pages, polished dashboards

### 2. Apple

- Local file: [apple/DESIGN.md](./examples/apple/DESIGN.md)
- Why it fits: Excellent for product hero restraint, cinematic whitespace, and single-accent discipline.
- Best for: premium landing pages, product launches, high-trust service brands

### 3. Linear

- Local file: [linear.app/DESIGN.md](./examples/linear.app/DESIGN.md)
- Why it fits: Calm, serious, dark-mode-native product UI with strong hierarchy and little noise.
- Best for: internal apps, dashboards, workspaces, dark product surfaces

### 4. Notion

- Local file: [notion/DESIGN.md](./examples/notion/DESIGN.md)
- Why it fits: Warm minimalism without losing professionalism; more human than sterile systems.
- Best for: productivity apps, content-heavy interfaces, warm premium landing pages

### 5. Cal.com

- Local file: [cal/DESIGN.md](./examples/cal/DESIGN.md)
- Why it fits: Monochrome restraint, strong typography, and confident product clarity without gimmicks.
- Best for: scheduling flows, straightforward SaaS pages, premium service businesses

### 6. HashiCorp

- Local file: [hashicorp/DESIGN.md](./examples/hashicorp/DESIGN.md)
- Why it fits: Strong enterprise seriousness, structural clarity, and operational credibility.
- Best for: infrastructure products, B2B apps, admin surfaces, technical dashboards

## Tier 2: Strong Conditional Picks

These are excellent, but they should be used more intentionally.

### 7. Stripe

- Local file: [stripe/DESIGN.md](./examples/stripe/DESIGN.md)
- Why it fits: World-class premium credibility and polished fintech hierarchy.
- Watch-out: It can pull a project toward fintech elegance and purple-heavy brand cues if copied too literally.
- Best for: trust-heavy landing pages, payments, B2B marketing, proof-rich pages

### 8. Raycast

- Local file: [raycast/DESIGN.md](./examples/raycast/DESIGN.md)
- Why it fits: Dark, premium, tool-like, and highly controlled.
- Watch-out: Strong personality can become too stylized if the product needs quieter trust.
- Best for: developer tools, desktop-style product pages, dark app shells

### 9. Resend

- Local file: [resend/DESIGN.md](./examples/resend/DESIGN.md)
- Why it fits: Editorial premium darkness with technical precision.
- Watch-out: The serif/editorial drama is strong; use it when you want luxury and clarity, not neutrality.
- Best for: premium technical brands, launch pages, email or communications products

### 10. Coinbase

- Local file: [coinbase/DESIGN.md](./examples/coinbase/DESIGN.md)
- Why it fits: Clean institutional trust and strong finance-grade clarity.
- Watch-out: It can feel too financial or corporate for warmer products.
- Best for: trust-heavy businesses, finance, compliance, local-service trust surfaces

### 11. Claude

- Local file: [claude/DESIGN.md](./examples/claude/DESIGN.md)
- Why it fits: Warm, editorial, intelligent, and calm.
- Watch-out: Better for thoughtful and human-centered products than cold operational dashboards.
- Best for: AI products, editorial products, warm premium brands

### 12. Mintlify

- Local file: [mintlify/DESIGN.md](./examples/mintlify/DESIGN.md)
- Why it fits: Clean docs clarity, white-space discipline, and developer-friendly polish.
- Watch-out: It is documentation-forward, so it should shape structure more than brand personality.
- Best for: docs, developer products, onboarding surfaces, knowledge-heavy apps

## Not Default For This Founder

These are not bad references. They are just less aligned with the founder's default taste.

- `Lovable`: too playful and gradient-heavy for the default premium-professional direction
- `Cohere`: stronger gradient/data-aesthetic than the founder usually wants
- `Framer`: very design-forward, but can drift too flashy or motion-led as a default
- `Miro`: brighter and more energetic than the founder's calm default
- `Zapier`: friendly and useful, but less premium-minimal than the founder's preference
- `Wise`: bold and high-energy, but less restrained than the founder's default mode

## Rule For Future Use

When using `awesome-design-md` for this founder:

1. Start with Tier 1.
2. Only use Tier 2 when the product context clearly calls for it.
3. Blend carefully instead of copying a single source literally.
4. Always run the result back through the founder taste profile before shipping.
