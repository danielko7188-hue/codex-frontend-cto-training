## First impression

- Strong: category-rich, commerce-native, confidence-building, buyer-first without feeling cheap
- Weak: can get visually busy if the product does not actually have marketplace depth

## What to borrow

- category exploration: the page quickly proves breadth through useful browsing paths
- trust mechanics: low minimums, returns, and payment terms reduce buyer hesitation
- value filters: women owned, eco-friendly, handmade, and other value-based paths help discovery feel intentional
- audience clarity: the page knows it is for retailers and keeps reinforcing that
- merchant confidence: risk-reduction is concrete, not vague

## What not to borrow

- dense category sprawl for smaller or simpler products
- search-heavy marketplace language when discovery is not the core job

## Why this matters

- It is a strong marketplace reference because it combines discovery, trust, and transactional confidence without collapsing into clutter.

## Reusable lesson

- Good marketplaces make discovery feel rich, but they win trust by making risk-reduction policies obvious and concrete.
