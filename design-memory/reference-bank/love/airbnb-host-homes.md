# Reference Entry

- Name: Airbnb Host Homes
- URL or source: https://www.airbnb.com/host/homes
- Product type: two-sided marketplace / host supply acquisition
- Bucket: love
- Date reviewed: 2026-04-07

## First impression

- Strong: friendly, low-friction, trust-forward, highly legible for a two-sided marketplace
- Weak: can feel too consumer-light if copied onto more operational B2B products

## What to borrow

- onboarding clarity: easy, worth it, and protected is a strong trust-building sequence
- anxiety reduction: the page answers fears before pushing harder conversion
- support framing: co-hosts, local guidance, and FAQ content make starting feel possible
- trust language: protection, fees, and regulations are addressed directly
- product proof: listing previews make the outcome tangible

## What not to borrow

- consumer softness for products that need more authority or enterprise confidence
- too much FAQ structure when the product needs a stronger hero and proof rhythm

## Why this matters

- It is a strong marketplace reference because it reduces supplier-side fear without losing conversion focus.

## Reusable lesson

- Marketplace onboarding gets stronger when the page resolves risk, effort, and trust in plain language before asking for commitment.
