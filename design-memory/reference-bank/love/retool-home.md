# Reference Entry

- Name: Retool
- URL or source: https://retool.com/
- Product type: internal tools / AI app platform
- Bucket: love

## First impression

- Strong: dense but legible, builder-first, practical, product-proof-heavy
- Weak: can skew too tool-centric for audiences that need more emotional positioning

## What to borrow

- product proof: put real interface surfaces on screen early
- density: show serious capability without collapsing into clutter
- AI framing: position AI as production help, not magic
- layout: organize the story by user type and real use case
- trust: security, auditability, and deployment options stay visible

## What not to borrow

- builder jargon for non-technical audiences
- dense app screenshots when the product is still too simple to support them

## Why this matters

- It is a strong reference for operator-density and AI-enabled product pages that still feel controlled.

## Reusable lesson

- Dense products feel trustworthy when the page shows real working surfaces and explains exactly who they are for.
