# Reference Entry

- Name: Notion Sites
- URL or source: https://www.notion.com/product/sites
- Product type: website builder / publishing tool
- Bucket: love
- Date reviewed: 2026-04-07

## First impression

- Strong: calm, direct, low-friction, editorially simple without feeling empty
- Weak: can feel almost too lightweight for products that need heavier trust or operational proof

## What to borrow

- hero clarity: the promise is plain, short, and easy to understand
- onboarding story: make, personalize, publish is a strong three-step structure
- visual restraint: the page stays useful without over-designing every surface
- product explanation: features read like real tasks, not abstract platform claims
- template showcase: examples prove range without making the page noisy

## What not to borrow

- ultra-light tone for products that need more weight, urgency, or authority
- minimalism that removes needed proof

## Why this matters

- It is a strong reminder that calm product pages still need a clear path from promise to action.

## Reusable lesson

- Simplicity works best when the product promise, setup steps, and examples are all obvious in the first few scrolls.
