## Main issues found

- The overall interface was cleaner than average, but still felt like a competent prototype rather than a premium product.
- The homepage hero had strong raw typography but weak proof rhythm and a less convincing product preview.
- The shared visual language leaned soft and beige in a way that risked flattening hierarchy.
- The dashboard was directionally right but still needed stronger contrast between workspace, rail, and supporting surfaces.

## Visual thesis

- Quiet operating software with stronger contrast, clearer proof, and a calmer but more intentional premium finish.

## What changed

- Strengthened the homepage composition and tightened CTA language.
- Reworked the preview window to feel like a product surface instead of a floating sample list.
- Sharpened shared visual tokens for surfaces, contrast, buttons, and rail treatment.
- Tightened intake headline behavior and fixed a mobile layout regression found during visual QA.

## Reusable lessons

- Premium often comes from strengthening the product preview and proof structure, not just repainting the hero.
- Local-service workflow products benefit from calm authority more than flashy startup cues.
- Beige or warm-neutral systems need sharper contrast discipline or they start flattening hierarchy.
