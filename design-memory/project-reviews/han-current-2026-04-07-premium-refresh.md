# Han Current Review — 2026-04-07

## Project type

- trust-heavy B2B OEM landing page
- bilingual English/Korean marketing and lead capture
- primary job: make U.S. buyers feel confident enough to request specs and MOQ

## Before

- layout had the right sections but felt generic and over-boxed
- typography was undersized and low-authority for a premium manufacturer
- aqua accents read more template-like than credible
- repeated grid sections diluted hierarchy instead of strengthening it
- reveal behavior hid too much content in visual QA and weakened perceived polish

## Direction used

- base: Apple restraint
- add: Coinbase-style trust clarity for CTA and business credibility
- founder taste match: minimalist, premium, professional, calm, proof-first

## What changed

- shifted the system toward warm neutrals, dark ink, and a single stronger blue accent
- upgraded the navigation and footer to feel more deliberate and premium
- rebuilt the hero around trust, proof, and a clearer OEM positioning
- improved the contact page so the form feels like a serious business inquiry surface
- removed fragile reveal-on-scroll behavior in favor of calmer mount animation

## Reusable lessons

- for trust-heavy OEM or service pages, proof and operational clarity matter more than decorative styling
- a calmer palette with stronger typography can do more for perceived quality than adding effects
- bilingual sites benefit from one disciplined system more than from trying to look flashy
- contact forms should feel like an executive intake, not a generic lead form
